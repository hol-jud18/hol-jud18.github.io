This is the first post. I'm starting this blog to document my malware development research as I go — 
the goal is to build a public record of what I'm working on and how I think through problems.

## why public

Two reasons. First, writing forces clarity. If I can't explain what I built and why, I don't fully 
understand it yet. Second, the security community runs on shared knowledge — nearly everything I've 
learned came from someone else's blog or paper. I want to contribute back.

## what i'll write about

Posts will fall into a few categories:

- **Technique breakdowns** — implementing a specific attack technique from scratch, explaining each piece
- **RE notes** — reversing something and documenting what I find
- **Research rabbit holes** — deep-dives into Windows internals, specific APIs, or undocumented behavior
- **Project posts** — building something larger and writing about the architecture and decisions

## format

I'm going for technical depth over polish. Code will be included. I'll note what didn't work as well as 
what did. The point is the process, not a cleaned-up tutorial.

Next post will be the first real technical one.
